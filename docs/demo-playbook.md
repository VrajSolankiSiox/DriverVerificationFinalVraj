# Demo Playbook — Rank Me Now

## Overview

Use this playbook when presenting a report to a hotel owner. The goal: **win the owner** by making them feel their current operations are inadequate compared to comps. Every section answers: *"Where are you losing to competitors?"*

---

## Step 1: Competitive Gap Summary (2 min)

**Slide:** Where You're Losing

**What to say:**
> "Here's where you're losing. Let's dig in. We've compared you to your compset across rate positioning, website, reviews, and SEO. The gaps are measurable."

**Talking points:**
- Lead with the strongest gap (rate, website, reviews, or SEO)
- If rate: "You're leaving revenue on the table—comps are holding rate where you're underpricing."
- If website: "Your site scores X vs comps at Y—guests are bouncing to [Comp A]."
- If reviews: "You're #4 of 5 on TripAdvisor. Comps are winning the consideration phase."
- If SEO: "Your site is invisible where comps rank. We can fix that."

---

## Step 2: Rate Positioning (3 min)

**Slide:** Pricing Positioning

**What to say:**
> "You're $X below comps on 20 nights. That's measurable revenue left on the table. This isn't a revenue claim—it's an outside-in observation of publicly available rates."

**Talking points:**
- Show the 90-day rate comparison chart
- Highlight nights where subject is lowest-priced
- "Comps are capturing more on similar dates. What's your pricing strategy for those windows?"

---

## Step 3: Website Audit (3 min)

**Slide:** Website Audit

**What to say:**
> "Your site scores 71 vs comps at 85. Guests are bouncing to [Comp A]. Here's what we see."

**Talking points:**
- Direct booking UX, content completeness, technical hygiene
- "Where does the booking CTA live? How visible are your offers?"
- "Comps have clearer contact and trust signals. Guests choose confidence."

---

## Step 4: Reviews (2 min)

**Slide:** Review Comparison (if available)

**What to say:**
> "You're #4 of 5 on TripAdvisor. Comps are winning the consideration phase. Reviews drive conversion before the guest ever visits your site."

**Talking points:**
- Compare subject vs comp ratings by source
- "What do you think is costing you when guests choose [Comp A] over you?"

---

## Step 5: SEO (2 min)

**Slide:** SEO Findings

**What to say:**
> "Your site is invisible where comps rank. We can fix that. Title tags, meta descriptions, schema markup—these are table stakes."

**Talking points:**
- SEO score vs comps
- "Your comps show up in local search. You don't. That's fixable."

---

## Step 6: 30/60/90 Action Plan (3 min)

**Slide:** Recommended Actions

**What to say:**
> "Here's what to do first. We can help operationalize. Validate pricing guardrails, tighten booking CTAs, close technical hygiene gaps."

**Talking points:**
- 30 days: Pricing, CTA placement, offer exposure
- 60 days: Weekday/weekend merchandising, market review cadence, technical hygiene
- 90 days: Cross-functional workflow, conversion tracking, compset expansion

---

## Close

**What to say:**
> "What's the one thing you'd fix first if you had the data to back it?"

**Goal:** Get the owner to commit to one action. Use the report as proof.

---

## Tips

- Never present the subject hotel in isolation. Always frame vs comps.
- Use "you" language: "You're losing," "Your site," "You're #4."
- Lead with the strongest gap. Don't bury it.
- If they push back: "Your comps are doing X; you're not. That gap is measurable."
